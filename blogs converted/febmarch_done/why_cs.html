Overview of the Online Master’s in Computer Science at Texas Wesleyan

We understand completing a graduate degree often means having to balance a range of responsibilities at once. That's why we strive to make this program as flexible and versatile as possible for busy professionals who are serious about advancing their education on their own schedule.

Program Structure and Focus Areas

Our master's in computer science program consists of a total of 30 credit hours, with coursework covering essential topics including:

  • Computer architecture
  • Machine learning
  • Database systems
  • Computer system security
  • Operating systems

With numerous start dates throughout the year, you can enroll and start taking classes sooner rather than later. Depending on whether you choose to enroll part or full time, you could earn your degree in as little as 18 months—and from there, you’ll be ready to stand out in today’s competitive computer science and tech job market.

Admissions Requirements and Prerequisites

For those interested in applying for our online master's in computer science degree program, be aware of some key admission requirements and prerequisites.

  • Hold a bachelor’s degree from a regionally accredited college or university (or a comparable foreign degree) in computer science or a related STEM field (science, technology, engineering, or mathematics).
  • Applicants with a non-related degree must complete prerequisite courses, including Calculus II and coursework in C++, Java, and Python. (Consider hyperlinking this sentence to your prerequisites page.)
  • Have a minimum 3.0 undergraduate GPA. Applicants with a GPA below 3.0 may submit GRE scores or demonstrate relevant work experience in the field.
  • Provide proof of English proficiency.
  • Submit a current CV/resume and official transcripts from all previously attended institutions.

Flexibility for Working Professionals

At Texas Wesleyan, we know that versatility is important in a graduate degree program, particularly in the demanding field of computer science. For this reason, we have worked hard to build an online master's in computer science program with self-paced learning options and convenient scheduling.

Self-Paced Learning Options

The format of our master's in computer science program is 100% online, meaning you'll never have to deal with the hassle of commuting to campus to attend classes. Likewise, classes hosted entirely online allow students the convenience of working at their own pace. This program relies on user-friendly and intuitive e-learning platforms, including the popular Canvas learning management system (LMS) — so logging in and accessing your classes is simple.

Classes in this program meet asynchronously, so you can enjoy being able to work from anywhere on your own time. This includes watching class lectures at times that are most convenient for you as well as completing assignments on your own schedule.

Balancing Work, Family and Studies

One notable challenge that working professionals often run into when pursuing a graduate degree is trying to gracefully juggle school, work, family and social life when there are simply not enough hours in the day.

Texas Wesleyan's online master’s in computer science strives to help students achieve a better work/life balance by offering flexible scheduling and asynchronous classes; this way, you can work your studies into your busy schedule as you see fit. If you're having a particularly busy week at work, you can scale back on your studies until things slow down a bit. On the other hand, you always have the option to work ahead and tackle some extra coursework when the opportunity arises. All of this flexibility enables you to maintain a healthier work/life/study balance during your time in school.

High-Demand Technical Skills and Certifications

What can you expect to learn in an online master's in computer science program at Texas Wesleyan University? As part of this degree program, you complete coursework in a range of topics that were chosen by our team to help you gain a competitive edge in the current job market. Networking and Industry Partnerships

Participation in our online Computer Science program also means having access to valuable industry partnerships and networking opportunities that could empower you to achieve your professional goals. Real-World Application and Experiential Learning

Another aspect of our computer science program that sets us apart from others? Project-based coursework and practical capstone projects that enable students to apply the theories and concepts they've learned about in class to real-world scenarios.

Project-Based Coursework

Courses throughout our master's in computer science program are designed to give students ample opportunities to directly apply what they've learned in class to hands-on experiences. Capstone Projects With Practical Impact

Finally, many of our degree programs include a capstone project, which allows students to explore a topic related to their field of study.
